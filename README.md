## What Is Kratom? A Quick Introduction

### Origin of the Kratom Plant
Kratom (*Mitragyna speciosa*) is a tropical evergreen tree in the coffee family, native to Southeast Asia. It has been used for centuries in countries like Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Myanmar. Traditional use involved chewing fresh leaves or brewing them into tea for energy during long workdays, or for relaxation and physical comfort in the evening.

### How Kratom Works
Kratom leaves contain over 40 naturally occurring alkaloids. The two most significant are mitragynine, which is the most abundant and primarily responsible for stimulating effects at lower doses, and 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH), which is present in much smaller quantities but is considerably more potent and associated with relaxation and pain relief. These alkaloids interact with opioid receptors in the brain, producing effects that vary dramatically depending on the dose, strain, and individual body chemistry.

## Why There Are Different Types of Kratom
Unlike cannabis, where different strains come from genetically distinct plant varieties, all kratom comes from the same species. The differences between kratom types are created primarily by when the leaves are harvested and how they are processed after harvest. The age of the leaf at picking, the drying method used (sun, indoor, or fermentation), and regional growing conditions all influence the final alkaloid profile. This means the same tree can produce different kratom types depending on how its leaves are handled.

## Kratom Alkaloid Profile: Why Different Types Feel Different

The reason different kratom types produce different effects comes down to their alkaloid content. **Mitragynine**, the primary alkaloid, makes up roughly 60 to 66 percent of the total alkaloid content in most kratom products. At lower doses, mitragynine acts as a stimulant, binding to adrenergic receptors and producing energy and focus. At higher doses, it engages opioid receptors more strongly, shifting toward sedation and physical comfort.

**7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH)** is present in much smaller quantities, typically less than 2 percent in natural leaf products, but it is roughly 13 times more potent than morphine at opioid receptors. The ratio between mitragynine and 7-OH largely determines a strain's character. Red vein strains tend to have relatively higher 7-OH concentrations due to their processing methods, contributing to their relaxing reputation. White vein strains, processed from younger leaves with minimal fermentation, tend to preserve more of the stimulating alkaloid profile.

Other alkaloids like **speciogynine**, **paynantheine**, and **speciociliatine** also contribute to the overall effect, though research on their individual roles is still emerging. This is why full-spectrum products, which preserve the natural range of alkaloids, often produce a more well-rounded experience than isolated extracts.

# How Kratom Is Harvested and Processed (And Why It Matters)

## Sun Drying vs Indoor Drying
The drying method is one of the most significant factors in determining a kratom product's final character. Sun-drying leaves for extended periods tends to produce lighter, more stimulating profiles associated with white vein products. Indoor air-drying preserves a more balanced alkaloid composition, resulting in green vein characteristics. The duration of exposure to sunlight, humidity levels, and temperature all influence the final result.

## The Fermentation Process
Fermentation is what creates red, gold, and bentuangie kratom. Leaves are sealed in bags or containers where natural moisture triggers oxidation and chemical changes in the alkaloid profile. Red kratom undergoes approximately three days of fermentation. Gold kratom uses a shorter fermentation period of about two days. Bentuangie involves an extended fermentation that produces its signature deep relaxation. The fermentation process is comparable to how tea processing creates different varieties from the same plant.

## How Processing Changes Effects
Research from ACS Laboratory in partnership with Mitra Science has confirmed that post-harvest processing directly impacts alkaloid concentrations. Fermentation can increase the relative concentration of certain alkaloids while reducing others. Heat-based processing may cause chemical transformations similar to how decarboxylation changes cannabinoids in cannabis. Seasonal and geographical factors also play a role, with studies showing that total alkaloid concentration in kratom leaves varies significantly between harvest seasons. This is why the same strain from the same vendor can vary slightly between batches.

# Types of Kratom Products Available

### Kratom Powder
Raw kratom powder is the most traditional and affordable product form. Dried leaves are ground into a fine powder that can be mixed into water, juice, or food. Powder offers the fastest onset among non-extract products (15 to 30 minutes when dissolved in liquid) and provides the most flexibility in dosing since you can measure exact amounts. The main drawbacks are the intensely bitter taste and the need for a scale to measure accurate doses.

### Kratom Capsules
Capsules contain pre-measured kratom powder enclosed in gelatin or vegetarian shells. They eliminate the bitter taste and provide consistent dosing without a scale. However, capsules take longer to break down in the stomach, resulting in a slower onset of 30 to 45 minutes. They also cost more per dose than bulk powder due to manufacturing and packaging.

### Kratom Gummies
[Kratom gummies](https://github.com/best-kratom/best-kratom-gummies) are infused with concentrated kratom extract and are one of the fastest-growing product categories. Gummies are the most beginner-friendly format but cost significantly more per dose than powder or capsules.

### Kratom Extracts and Shots
Extracts are concentrated forms of kratom that isolate and concentrate the primary alkaloids, particularly mitragynine. Liquid extract shots deliver the highest potency and fastest onset among commercial products. However, they carry the greatest risk of overconsumption and rapid tolerance buildup. The FDA has specifically targeted high-concentration 7-OH extract products with enforcement actions. Extract shots are not recommended for beginners and should be used with extreme caution even by experienced users.

### Kratom Tea
Brewing kratom powder or crushed leaves into tea is one of the most traditional consumption methods. The hot water extracts the alkaloids, and the liquid is strained and consumed. Tea offers a relatively fast onset and allows users to add honey, lemon, or other flavors to mask the bitterness. Some alkaloids may be reduced by heat, potentially producing a slightly different effect profile compared to consuming raw powder.

## Kratom Tolerance and Strain Rotation

Regular use of any single kratom type builds tolerance, requiring progressively higher doses to achieve the same effects. Strain rotation is the most widely recommended strategy for managing tolerance. The principle is simple: because different strains have slightly different alkaloid profiles, rotating between them prevents your body from fully adapting to any one profile.

A practical rotation schedule might look like this: Monday and Tuesday use a green strain, Wednesday and Thursday switch to white, Friday use red, and take the weekend off entirely. The American Kratom Association recommends not exceeding 8 grams of botanical kratom per day. Taking at least two full days off per week significantly helps maintain sensitivity. If you notice your usual dose becoming less effective, a tolerance break of five to seven days can reset your baseline.

## Are All Kratom Types Safe? What You Should Know

### Possible Side Effects
Common side effects across all kratom types include nausea, dizziness, constipation, dry mouth, and drowsiness. These are dose-dependent, meaning they are more likely and more severe at higher doses. Some users also report headaches, irritability, and increased heart rate, particularly with stimulating white strains at high doses. Starting with a low dose and increasing gradually is the most effective way to minimize side effects.

### FDA Position and Regulatory Concerns
The FDA has not approved kratom for any medical use and continues to advise consumers about potential risks including liver toxicity, seizures, and substance use disorder. In 2025, the FDA recommended that the DEA classify concentrated 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) products as Schedule I substances. Importantly, the FDA stated it is not currently focused on natural kratom leaf products, drawing a clear distinction between concentrated extracts and traditional leaf-based kratom. This distinction is becoming increasingly important in both federal and state-level policy discussions.

### Dependency Risk Across Different Types
Not all kratom types carry equal dependency risk. Higher-potency strains like **Maeng Da** and concentrated extract products carry more risk than milder options like **Red Bali** or **Green Malay** at standard doses. Extract shots and enhanced kratom products present the highest dependency risk due to their concentrated alkaloid content. Daily use of any type increases dependency risk regardless of strain. Withdrawal symptoms, while generally milder than those associated with prescription opioids, can include irritability, fatigue, muscle aches, insomnia, and anxiety.

### Who Should Avoid Kratom
Kratom should be avoided by pregnant or nursing individuals, people with liver conditions or epilepsy, those with a history of substance use disorder, and anyone taking medications that interact with kratom, particularly those processed by the liver. Individuals with heart conditions or mental health disorders should exercise particular caution. Mixing kratom with alcohol, benzodiazepines, or opioids is dangerous and should never be done. Always consult a healthcare professional before using kratom, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions.

## Legal Status of Kratom in 2026

Kratom remains federally unscheduled in the United States. The DEA considered emergency scheduling in 2016 but withdrew the proposal following significant public opposition. However, the legal landscape at the state level is complex and rapidly changing.

As of early 2026, kratom is fully banned in seven states: **Alabama**, **Arkansas**, **Indiana**, **Louisiana** (effective August 2025), **Rhode Island** (with a new regulation act passed in 2025 taking effect April 2026), **Vermont**, and **Wisconsin**. Washington D.C. also prohibits sales. In **California**, the Department of Public Health has been actively removing kratom and 7-OH products from shelves under the Sherman Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act, despite no specific state law banning the substance.

On the regulation side, multiple states have adopted **Kratom Consumer Protection Acts (KCPAs)** that regulate rather than ban kratom. States with active KCPA frameworks include **Arizona**, **Utah**, **Georgia**, **Nevada**, **Oklahoma**, **Oregon**, **Texas**, **Colorado**, **Virginia**, and **West Virginia**. These laws typically require age restrictions (18 or 21+), product labeling standards, testing mandates, and limits on adulteration.

The 2026 legislative season has brought new challenges. **South Carolina**, which passed a KCPA in 2025, is now facing bills that would repeal it and schedule kratom. **Utah** is debating a bill to repeal its pioneering KCPA. **Iowa** has multiple ban bills advancing through committees. **Ohio** issued emergency guidance targeting certain mitragynine-related compounds while stating that natural kratom in leaf form was not targeted. The distinction between natural leaf kratom and concentrated 7-OH products continues to shape the national debate. Always verify your state and local laws before purchasing, as the landscape can shift within months.
